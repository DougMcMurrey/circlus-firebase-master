# Associated Emails

## Edit Links

Every contact on Circlus has a unique, secure link associated with it made of a randomly generated string of numbers and letters. Anybody who visits that link will reach a webpage where they can edit that contact. (If you've ever shared a link in Google Docs, this is the same thing!)

Example: A shortened version of an Edit Link. The real ones are even longer.

```
https://circl.us/c/a5734fded6/e/7bd53e255059fb61...eef1395457fd4c01
```

As a club administrator, you have access to all these Edit Links. To copy one to a clipboard, click on the Link icon above a contact you are editing. 🔗

Paste it into your browser to see what that link gives access to.

As the club administrator, you can send these links to anyone you want, or to no-one at all. It's up to you who gets to edit what in your club!

## Distributing Edit Links

So now that we have edit links, getting your club members to all have control of their own info should be as easy as:

- Making a blank contact for each club member
- Sending each club member their own, unique edit link

Right? Thankfully, Circlus has a way to do this automatically, called _Associated Emails_.

When editing a contact, at the top is a Section labeled "Associated Email," above the name and picture. You can click on it to add or change it.

<div class="img-caption center">

![Contact With Associated Email](/_media/assoc_email_002.png ':class=s-350')

_Above: The associated email. Below: The contact's 2 public emails._

</div>

!> **_Remember:_** Associated Emails are **not** a part of the contact's data. They won't show up if you print your directory or export a contact to a VCard, and **cannot** be seen on your club's public directory.

The best part about Circlus is not having to do manual entry yourself. With only a list of your club members' emails, you can put them to work filling out the info they know best: their own.

_But_, in order to do that, Circlus needs to know how to contact them before you enter any contact information.

- You (the administrator) add a list of email addresses to your club.
- Circlus automatically creates an empty contact associated to each email address.
- Circlus automatically sends a unique email to each address, containing a secure link unique to each of them.
- Each person can now edit their contact information — and nobody else's.

The purpose of Associated Emails is to be able to send edit links to the right people - that's all!

In a future update, club administrators may be able to send newsletters out to all the associated emails in a club.
